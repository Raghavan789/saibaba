About

Sai Baba of Shirdi, also known as Shirdi Sai Baba, was an Indian spiritual master who was regarded by his devotees as a saint, fakir, and satguru. He was revered by both his Hindu and Muslim devotees during, as well as after his lifetime.

Born in the late 19th century, Sai Baba's real name, birthplace, and date of birth are unknown. He spent most of his life in Shirdi, a town in Maharashtra, India.

Sai Baba's teachings combined elements of Hinduism and Islam. He gave no distinction based on religion or caste. His philosophy was based on love, forgiveness, helping others, charity, contentment, inner peace, and devotion to God and guru.

Even today, Sai Baba is revered by millions across the world. His teachings continue to inspire and guide people towards a life of spiritual awareness and social harmony.
